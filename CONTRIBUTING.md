# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

# Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Showing empathy toward other community members.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and community.
- Helping newcomers learn and contribute.
- Encouraging collaboration and knowledge sharing.
- Maintaining professionalism in discussions and code reviews.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct.
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments.
- Trolling, inflammatory remarks, or disruptive behavior.
- Publishing private information without consent.
- Deliberate intimidation or threats.
- Posting spam, malicious code, or misleading content.
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional environment.

# Contributor Responsibilities

Contributors are expected to:

- Follow project guidelines and coding standards.
- Write clear and maintainable code.
- Test changes before submitting pull requests.
- Respect feedback from maintainers and reviewers.
- Help maintain a positive and productive community.
- Report issues responsibly.
- Keep discussions focused on project improvement.

---

# Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Reviewing contributions fairly and respectfully.
- Providing constructive feedback.
- Enforcing this Code of Conduct consistently.
- Taking appropriate action when violations occur.
- Promoting an inclusive and welcoming environment.
- Ensuring project discussions remain professional.

Maintainers reserve the right to remove, edit, or reject contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

---

# Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the project maintainers.

When reporting an issue, include:

- A description of the incident.
- Relevant screenshots or logs, if available.
- Names of individuals involved (if applicable).
- Any additional context that may help investigate.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and confidentially whenever possible.

---

# Enforcement Guidelines

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in actions including:

### 1. Warning

A private warning describing the violation and expected behavior.

### 2. Temporary Restriction

Temporary suspension from discussions, issues, pull requests, or other project activities.

### 3. Permanent Ban

Permanent removal from project participation for serious or repeated violations.

The severity of enforcement actions will depend on the nature and frequency of the violation.
